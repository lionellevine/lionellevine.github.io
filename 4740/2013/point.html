Three good books:

  • Poisson processes by Kingman (defines Poisson process on much more general spaces)
  • Introduction to random matrices, by Anderson, Guionnet and Zeitouni (The eigenvalues of a random matrix are a point process with repulsion: not Poisson)
  • Gaussian analytic functions by Hough, Krishnapur, Peres and Virág (The zeros of a random power series are another point process with repulsion)

Comparison of a 2-dimensional Poisson point process with the zeros of a Gaussian analytic function. Since the zeros repel each another, so they look much more evenly spread out than the Poisson points!
